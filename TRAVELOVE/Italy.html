Travel Guide









Italy with her unrivalled beauty and baffling contradictions, continues to seduce and enchant those who are drawn to her. And the Italian way of life is famous the world over, with style and character drawn from centuries of culture and history, amid breathtaking scenery and a desirable Mediterranean climate.



Popular places

Florence is one of the world’s great historic cities, packed with architectural masterpieces, palaces and art galleries – an essential Italian destination in its own right.

Venice charms, captivates and seduces with its dazzling and unique beauty, encapsulated in its enchanting buildings and picturesque waterways.

Milan and Turin, two utterly contemporary cities packed with art and history. You could follow the path of generations of travellers who, with Dante and Ariosto in hand, toured the cities of Lombardy, the Veneto, Emilia-Romagna and Tuscany. If you want to catch your breath and relax, retreat into the green hills of Umbria, home of Italy’s beloved St Francis of Assisi.

Things to See



Venice Take in the broad sweep of the Grand Canal from a No. 1 vaporetto (water bus) or a romantic gondola; or hop on a ferry to see the islands of Murano, Burano or Torcello.

Portofino Promontory This coastal reserve in Liguria embraces a chic resort, a marine reserve, and pine and olive groves on rugged slopes.

Parco Nazionale Gran Paradiso An area of outstanding natural beauty, this Alpine park in the Italian Graian Alps is fabulous trekking country.

Lake Como Arguably Italy’s loveliest lake, Como promises gorgeous villas and gardens, charming ports of call and mouthwatering fish dinners.

Le Cinque Terre A cluster of five fishing villages clinging to the rocky coastline along a stretch of protected parkland laced with cliffside walking trails which offer sweeping views over terraced olive groves.

Food



Cannoli Yum! Cannoli comes from Sicily. These are pastries filled with ricotta. There are candied oranges and cherries at the end of a cannoli. It is such a lovely comfort street food. Just grab a cannoli and you will be satisfied for the rest of the day.

Lampredotto The origin of this street food is Florence. Have you even heard of it? Italians have such cool recipes which we do not even know of. The Lampredotto is cow stomach which is cooked in a broth which has tomato and herbs. It is usually served on a bun and tastes amazing. This is true comfort food and you will be wanting more and more.

Panini There is nothing better than a classic Italian Panini. It has fresh ingredients sandwiched between two panini breads. Prosciutto, tomatoes, mozzarella cheese and basil are the most classic ingredients in a panini. Just grab a panini and you will be tummy full feeling like a happy and satisfied soul. In Italian, Panini means bread roll.

Trapizzino This is another famous street food from Rome in Italy. This has been around for few years but it has gained much popularity in the recent years. It was invented in the year 2008 by Chef Stefano. The Trapizzino is white pizza with yummy fillings like pepper, chicken or oxtail. You will find the best Trapizzino in Rome. When in Rome, do eat this!

Arrancini Arrancini is a much loved street food of Italy. These are basically rice balls which are deep fried. Street food is all about comfort food and nothing is better than deep fried food. The Arrancini is basically a risotto which is stuffed with peas, minced meat and cheese. Now imagine this combination turning into rice balls and being deep fried. The end product is deliciousness. You will find the best Arrancini is Sicily in Italy.
